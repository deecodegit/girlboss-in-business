## STEP 1: DEFINE THE COMPANY CORRECTLY

☐ What problem does it really solve?  
☐ Who is the real customer vs user?  
☐ What job is being outsourced to the company?

STEP 2: IDENTIFY BUSINESS MODELS (MULTIPLE)  
☐ Primary business model  
☐ Secondary / hidden models  
☐ What model do customers think it is vs what it actually is?

STEP 3: MAP THE VALUE FLOW  
☐ Who pays?  
☐ Who delivers?  
☐ Who takes risk?  
☐ Where is margin created?

STEP 4: UNIT ECONOMICS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)  
☐ Revenue per unit (order/user/month)  
☐ Variable costs per unit  
☐ Contribution margin  
☐ Fixed costs  
☐ Break-even logic

STEP 5: REVENUE & PRICING LOGIC  
☐ Pricing power level  
☐ Discounts vs value pricing  
☐ Cross-subsidies  
☐ Hidden monetization (ads, data, float)

STEP 6: COST STRUCTURE & SCALE EFFECTS  
☐ What scales linearly?  
☐ What scales exponentially?  
☐ Where does operating leverage kick in?

STEP 7: GROWTH ENGINE  
☐ Acquisition channels  
☐ Retention mechanisms  
☐ Network effects (yes/no/type)  
☐ Switching costs

STEP 8: COMPETITIVE MOAT  
☐ Brand  
☐ Cost advantage  
☐ Data  
☐ Regulation  
☐ Ecosystem lock-in  
STEP 9: RISK & FAILURE POINTS  
☐ Regulatory risk  
☐ Margin compression  
☐ Customer fatigue  
☐ Dependency risk (partners, ads, funding)

STEP 10: STRATEGIC TRADE-OFFS  
☐ What are they not doing on purpose?  
☐ What would break if they changed direction?

STEP 11: FUTURE OPTIONS  
☐ New revenue streams  
☐ Cost reduction levers  
☐ Adjacent expansions  
☐ Exit or consolidation paths

STEP 12: SYNTHESIS (THE REAL TEST)  
Write 5 lines max:

* What model wins here?  
* Why this company?  
* Why now?  
* Why it could fail?  
* One bold strategic move you’d make
